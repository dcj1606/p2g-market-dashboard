07 Impact assessment for Parcel2Go Current month lens
🔴 Volume suppressors — active
  • Sharp retail sales decline (−1.2% MoM) driven by poor weather
  • Consumer confidence fell to −19 · unemployment at 5.2% post-pandemic high
  • Non-food discretionary weakest · fewest parcels per £ spent
  • Retail insolvencies elevated — fewer active sellers in market
  • Mortgage costs adding ~£1,500/yr · tightening household budgets
🟡 Structural headwinds — ongoing
  • Amazon self-delivery >50% — volume leaving broker-accessible pool
  • Evri-DHL merger = larger, better-capitalised direct SME competitor
  • OBR downgraded 2026 GDP from 1.4% to 1.1% (Spring Statement)
  • HMRC targeting ~4m online sellers · compliance drag on casual senders
  • CPI above 3% · inflation eroding real wages and spend capacity
🟢 Tailwinds — holding firm
  • Online penetration now 28.2% vs 26.7% a year ago — structural uplift
  • January 2026 was the strongest month since May 2024 (+1.8% MoM)
  • C2C / recommerce growing steadily (Vinted, Depop, eBay resellers)
  • OOH locker network expansion opening new send and receive points
  • YoY retail volumes still positive across most months (see chart 02)
📌 Smart Send cohort — watch points
  • Bad weather → more online shopping → potential parcel uplift signal
  • Jan discounts / clearance likely pulled some Feb volume forward
  • HMRC digital seller scrutiny rising — admin burden increasing for SMEs
  • Cost stack higher in 2026: NI, NLW, energy all up from April
  • Easter (30 Mar–6 Apr) is next volume catalyst — watch closely
